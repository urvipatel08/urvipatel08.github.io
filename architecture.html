Sheet A-01

Streets as Public Spaces and Drivers of Urban Development

2024 · Vandematram Road, Gota, Ahmedabad · Architectural Thesis

Overview

Vandematram Road is a 1.2-km arterial in Gota, Ahmedabad — a 30-metre right-of-way operating as a transit route when it could also serve as a public asset. The thesis begins with close observation: mapping building use, road hierarchy, street activity, and the informal ways vendors, pedestrians, auto-rickshaws, and cyclists actually claim the space.

The redesign redistributes the right-of-way across three zones, inserting a 2.5-metre walkway, a separate cycle track, formal vending space, shaded seating, and landscaped buffers. Vendor count grows from 85 to 150 in dedicated stalls; tree canopy from 30% to 70%.

Key Strategies

  • Reclaiming Public Space
  • Pedestrianization & Walkability
  • Organized Street Vending
  • User Safety & Accessibility

Programme

VENDORS85 existing → 150 proposed formal vending spaces
TREES30% existing canopy → 70% proposed tree coverage
LENGTH1.2 km redesigned in three distinct intervention zones

SITE — Vandematram Road, Gota, Ahmedabad · 1.2 km · 30 m ROW · 2024

Sheet A-02

Tackling the Urban Junction

Urban Design Studio · Naroda Gam Junction, Naroda, Ahmedabad

Overview

Naroda Gam Junction sits on Ahmedabad's north-eastern fringe — 7.1 km from the airport, 1.1 km from Naroda railway station, served by AMTS and BRTS transit, and connected via National Highway 48 and the S.P. Ring Road. Despite that connectivity, the junction operates as a collision point with no pedestrian infrastructure and public space designed for nobody in particular.

The project reads the junction through figure-ground analysis, building height, and land-use mapping, then proposes two complementary interventions: a Public Plaza anchored by a central fountain, and an adjacent Market building that formalises vendor activity in covered stalls.

Key Strategies

  • Designated Vendor Space
  • Public Plaza Anchoring
  • Defined Street Section
  • Transit-Oriented Access

Site Context

TRANSITAMTS bus, BRTS rapid transit and MYBYK cycle-share stops nearby
HIGHWAYNational Highway 48 and S.P. Ring Road regional connectivity
ECOLOGYKariya Lake and Ghodasar Canal as ecological and public space opportunity

SITE — Naroda Gam Junction, Naroda, Ahmedabad · near Kariya Lake · NH-48

Sheet A-03

Public Housing Re-development

Housing Studio · Lakeview Apartments, Vastrapur, Ahmedabad

Overview

Lakeview Apartments in Vastrapur is a 1990s private-public housing complex: 27 ground-plus-two blocks, 204 units, and roughly 800 residents on an 18-metre road with BRTS access 1.2 km away and a Metro station 1.7 km out. After thirty-plus years, the complex is structurally outdated with undersized units and inadequate green space.

The redevelopment consolidates the 27 dispersed low-rise blocks into six larger buildings, increasing total unit count to 280 while expanding unit areas and freeing ground-level area for landscaping and shared amenity space.

Key Strategies

  • Strategic Densification
  • Diverse Unit Typology
  • Ground-Level Activation
  • Transit-Adjacent Siting

SITE — Lakeview Apartments, Vastrapur, Ahmedabad · BRTS 1.2 km · Metro 1.7 km
