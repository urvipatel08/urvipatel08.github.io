Sheet UD-01

Urban Recalibration — Activating the Franklin Music Hall District

2025 · Callowhill, Philadelphia · Mixed-Use Master Plan

Overview

Located in the Callowhill neighborhood just north of the Vine Street Expressway, this project reimagines three underutilized industrial blocks anchored by Franklin Music Hall. Large warehouse structures, fragmented lots, and surface parking currently dominate a site at the edge of Center City, yet one that remains largely inactive and disconnected from surrounding neighborhood life despite its location.

The proposal introduces a mixed-use urban framework that leverages Franklin Music Hall as a cultural anchor. Green infrastructure, improved circulation networks, and carefully distributed building typologies repair fragmented urban edges, reduce the urban heat island effect, and activate the public realm through a series of connected green spaces and pedestrian corridors.

Through Wallacei multi-objective optimization, 7,500 massing configurations were generated and evaluated across four performance objectives: FAR, GAR, solar comfort, and view access, to arrive at a final form that balances density with openness, and repositions this industrial corridor as a connected, climate-responsive, and culturally active urban district.

Design Principles

  • Cultural Anchoring
  • Continuous Connectivity
  • Structured Density Distribution
  • Multi-Directional Permeability
  • Mixed-Use Intensification

Design Objectives

FARMaximize high-density development
GARMaximize green space integration
SOLARMaximize solar comfort at ground level
VIEWSMaximize view access to Franklin Music Hall, Center City and the Delaware River

SITE — N 5th to N 8th Street · Callowhill, Philadelphia PA 19123 · 3 city blocks

Sheet UD-02

The Water's Edge: Designing with Water

2025 · 501 N Christopher Columbus Blvd, Philadelphia · Waterfront Masterplan

Overview

A climate-responsive redevelopment proposal for Philadelphia's eastern waterfront at 501 N Christopher Columbus Boulevard. The project addresses the site's unique opportunities — its expansive river edge, aging infrastructure, flooding exposure, and dramatic skyline and river views — transforming a post-industrial stretch into a high-performing urban district that balances density with ecological function.

Through Wallacei multi-objective optimization, 2,500 massing scenarios across 50 generations were generated and analyzed for performance, resulting in a final configuration that balances density with openness, built form with ecology, and urban experience with resilience. High FAR zones cluster closer to Columbus Boulevard, while elevated green terraces and publicly accessible river decks soften the transition to the water.

Design Typologies

  • Floating Ecologies
  • Elevated Green
  • Lifted Edge

Design Objectives

FARDetermine built volume while preserving open space and ecological edge
GAROptimize landscape resilience along the Delaware River
WAREnsure development does not privatize the waterfront public realm
VIEWSEnsure density does not compromise visual connection to the water

SITE — 501 N Christopher Columbus Blvd, Philadelphia PA · Delaware River Waterfront

Sheet UD-03

Pedestrian Safety + Walkability

2026 · Ridge Avenue, Roxborough, Philadelphia · Corridor Redesign

Overview

Ridge Avenue is a vital neighborhood corridor in Roxborough, serving as both a transportation route and a community spine. Despite its importance, the avenue faces challenges related to pedestrian safety, walkability, and street accessibility. This project undertakes a comprehensive analysis of existing street conditions and the surrounding neighborhood to understand movement patterns, safety concerns, and everyday user experiences.

The design process was grounded in community engagement — shared meetings with residents, local stakeholders, and key contributors to neighborhood development. Community insights, combined with on-site observations and data-driven analysis, informed a people-centred redesign of Ridge Avenue that prioritises pedestrian safety, improved walkability, and inclusive street design.

Design Principles

  • Human-Centered Design
  • Complete Street Approach
  • Green & Sustainable Infrastructure
  • Community Identity & Placemaking
